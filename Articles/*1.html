A Guide to Posting

Meme Watch
March 26, 2017
The submission page can be found here, but we urge you to continue reading if you haven't already.

So, you want to write something. Of course you do. But you won't stop there. No, you'll have to share it with the entire internet in order to fill the gaping hole in your life that requires attention, in order to make yourself feel as if you will be remembered. (But seriously, if the previous sentence describes you, seek help.) Don't worry. It's normal. (While this feeling may be normal, it doesn't have to be. Ask for help immediately.) This guide aims to help you share your knowledge.

How to Write Something

In order to post an article, you'll need to write one first. This can be done on any text editor including the Reddit submission page.

How to Write Something Worth Reading

What makes a text great? What keeps the viewer reading? Here at Meme Watch, we've disected articles and found out what makes them worth reading. Here are some things we've found out

  • Begin with something enticing. In order to grab potential readers, you'll need a hook and a clear outline of what you're covering in the article.
  • Organization. You have to make things easy for the reader. They're not going to read your article if it jumps around. If you're article allows it, start with a problem, and present a probable solution. Most of the time, this method won't fit as the article types may not make it reasonable. But still, try not to jump around from topic to topic without providing a transition. Read your work at least one aloud in order to determine if it "flows" from topic to topic.
  • Dank meme references. You'll need a few of them, as these references bring viewers back time and time again.
  • Evidence. You can't claim anything without sources to build your theories off of. Just because you "feel" something doesn't mean that others will be swayed by this feeling. Sources can be things like scholarly articles, interviews, charts, and quotes.

How to Write Something that Won't Get Removed

Sharing your work allows yourself and others to gain from it, something that isn't possible if it gets blocked. At Meme Watch, we take a few issues very seriously. If your article dosen't fit these rules, then it will be removed immediately.

  • No Predjudism. If your article targets a race, ethnic group, organization, religion, the LGBT community, or a specific group of people negativley, then consider it gone. Our differences shouldn't be something that we are ashamed of. The Meme Economy will not function if only one group controls it.
  • Nothing NSFW. You'll notice we don't have an NSFW catagory. We don't plan on getting one. If your article features anything NSFW, then bye bye.
  • No Articles Lacking Quality. In other words, no shitposting. If an effort isn't visable in your post, consider it gone.
  • No Irrelevant Content. Something that is relevant to Meme Watch must help others with the Meme Economy and the Meme Economy alone.
  • No Excessive Swearing. Swearing is a tool used to refine human languages by demonstrating a certain emotion quickly. If your article uses a swear, that's fine. This isn't Kindergarten. But if your article excessivley uses swearing, then the point is lost, and so is your article from the site.

The Submission Process

Step One: Write Something

Writing something can be hard, but just keep on going!

In order to post an article, you'll need to write one first. This can be done on any text editor including the Reddit submission page.

Step Two: Submit to Reddit

Submitting to Reddit can be very difficult. Make sure to follow the following steps very carfully.

In order to be able to submit via Reddit, you must follow these rules exactly as stated.

  1. Your title must be the same as the article title.
  2. Your submission must be properly formatted. To learn how to do this, go to formatting.

Formatting

Formatting is what makes your articles look great, and show up on Meme Watch. In order to easily allow others to write and post to Meme Watch, we use basic HTML tags and classes. If you have had any previous experience with HTML, this should be fairly easy. If not, remember that you have access to many resources to help you. To look at what these tags show up as in a natural enviorment, go to our article template. To see if what you've created will work, go to this link and click "Run". Then, follow the posted instructions.

After checking your code, copy and paste the code onto Reddit, title your submission the name of your article, and submit. The article will show up shortly.
